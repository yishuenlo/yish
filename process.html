Process & System for

Habit Building

The intent of this website is to document my habit building process. In James Clear's Atomic Habit book, in order to develop a habit, it's important to consistenly show up in good days and bad days, to have accountability, and to make it satisfying.

The existence of this website is to address accountability. By documenting my progress to the world, I am holding myself accountable on my habit building.

To make it satisfying, I've developed a system to help me earn expensive toys I've always wanted, but could never justisfy the purchases: MacBook Pro and Wacom Cintiq Pro.

Using RPG game as a metaphor, MacBook Pro and Cintiq Pro are the fancy weapons for high level players. And I am slowly earning my experience and leveling up so I could wield these weapons to their fullest potential. And also I don't just spend a shit ton of money and just use them once or twice, but actually consistenly using them because I've build a habit of coding and painting already. ;)

Habit System

So here is how this works: for everyday I showed up and work on my habit, I will put a certain amount of money into my savings for my rewards. In this case, MacBook Pro and Cintiq Pro. The amount of money I earn is based on the phases of my habit process.

Habit Phasing

  • Phase 1: Code/paint for at least 10 mins ($1)
  • Phase 2: Code/paint for at least 30 mins ($2)
  • Phase 3: Code/paint for at least an hour ($3)
  • Phase 4: TBD ($4)
  • Phase 5: TBD ($5)

With this system, I am building my habit and skills, and earn the right to use my expensive toys. And yes, I also intentionally picked very expensive toys because it'd take me at least 2 years to practice these habits consistently, so by the time I do receive my toys, I'd be amazing at coding and digital painting already (I think... I hope... or maybe just not suck.)

To proceed to the next phase, I plan to do quarterly review to reflect on my progress and see if I am ready to move onto the next phase, and if I will need to make any adjustments to my habit building. This is why phase 4 and phase 5 are TBD, because I am not sure what is practical at this point. I will need more time to think about them and update them in the future.
